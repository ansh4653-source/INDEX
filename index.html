Digital Literacy

Digital literacy refers to the ability to effectively and critically use digital tools, platforms, and information. It helps individuals access, evaluate, create, and communicate information using technology.

  • Understanding digital devices and applications
  • Identifying reliable and fake information
  • Using technology for education and productivity

Digital Citizenship

Digital citizenship means practicing responsible, ethical, and respectful behavior while using digital technologies. A good digital citizen understands rights, responsibilities, and online etiquette.

  • Respecting others online
  • Following cyber laws and platform rules
  • Promoting positive online communication

Digital Footprint

A digital footprint is the permanent record of a person’s online activities. It includes social media posts, search history, comments, and shared content.

  • Active footprint – information shared intentionally
  • Passive footprint – data collected automatically
  • Careless footprints can affect future careers and reputation

Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying is the use of digital platforms to harass, threaten, or humiliate individuals. It can cause serious emotional and psychological harm.

  • Occurs on social media, gaming platforms, and messaging apps
  • Includes spreading rumors and abusive messages
  • Should be reported immediately

Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity involves protecting systems, networks, and personal data from cyber threats such as hacking, malware, and identity theft.

  • Use strong and unique passwords
  • Install antivirus and security updates
  • Avoid clicking on suspicious links
